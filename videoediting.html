For Professor Craig Duff's JOUR 390: Producing and Editing Social Media Video, I produced, reported and edited two 3 - 4 minute videos on Adobe Premiere Pro and Adobe After Effects. Following the class, I applied to become the Video Desk Editor at the Daily Northwestern for the Spring 2021 quarter.


Social Media Video


During Fall Quarter, I decided to take this course with Craig Duff due to my interest in video work. At this time, we were under COVID rules and regulations, and I was one of the few students who was taking the class remotely. Despite my worries, I took advantage of the stories present in my hometown, and with the equipment Prof. Duff sent to me, I was able to create entertaining videos.

We were tasked with producing two longer-form videos by the middle and end of the quarter. Our first project was to create an explainer-style video, and after pitching various ideas, I decided to do mine on the urge to reopen the Disneyland in Anaheim. I shot in-person footage myself at a rally that had taken place the weekend before, created engaging animations and interviewed key stakeholders for the story. For our final project, I also created a longer explainer-style video about finding love under COVID conditions. In order to break up the interviews and animations, I brainstormed ideas for b-roll by creating simple stop motions and filming friends.

My initial storyboards for my two projects, drawn on Procreate

Once I storyboarded, I went ahead with contacting sources that I found on various social media channels like a Disney fanpage on Facebook and app developers on TikTok. For b-roll, I created animations on Adobe After Effects after creating graphics on Adobe Illustrator. While it was quite a labor-intensive project, I was very happy with the video I had produced.

Since this course, I became more interested in producing and editing video coursework. I continued my efforts to better my skills in video by partaking in The Daily Northwestern and have offered up this skillset at various internships. I reached out to the Art Director at Vox Media and heard about their journey, and now I aspire to also produce similar explainer-style videos in my career.

The Daily Northwestern


Following this class, I looked for more opportunities to produce video at The Daily Northwestern. Throughout the devo process, I produced two videos in collaboration with the desk editors at the time. I followed up with creating a video on how prospective students are demonstrating interest in Northwestern during COVID times. In the spring quarter, I was encouraged to apply for the Video Desk Editor position, which I was fortunate enough to receive. As a result, I led my own video staff, which I assisted by watching multiple cuts and offering feedback on story flow and production edits. At the same time, I produced stories of my own and edited town halls about the vaccine for the student body.
