Technology and Human Interaction


In this course, I learned a variety of techniques often used in Human-Computer Interaction. For example, I performed a contextual inquiry, which combines interviewing the user about a procedure while also observing their surroundings. Through open-ended questions and observations, I determined the pain points of my roommate's photo-editing process and then brainstormed potential areas of improvement for Lightroom. Another major concept that this course went over was conducting a heuristic evaluation. Based on Nielsen’s list of 10 heuristics, rules of thumb for user interface design, I analyzed the Fandango website interface for errors within these heuristics and determined the severity of the issues. In addition, based on these criteria, I also determined possible fixes such as utilizing a pop-up effect or adding small thumbnails to remind the consumer of what the website focuses on.

Building off these research techniques, I worked on a group project for which our goal was to create a prototype for a new streaming service that would allow users to find personalized, real-time recommendations and connect with friends with commentary about their viewing history, connecting characteristics of a traditional streaming application with a social media application. Through user interviews and testing with initial sketches, we were able to build a working prototype on Figma that used the various heuristics we went over in class and also addressed our users’ concerns.

Knight Lab


Every quarter, the Knight Lab pulls together multidisciplinary teams of students together to collaborate on projects that they believe is important for the future of media. I had the fortunate experience of being chosen to be part of the 5-member team to work on the project "Augmented Reality Features with 3D Food Photography." Throughout the quarter, we experimented with AR and VR and how it can be implemented to showcase various food products. My team and I worked to create an immersive experience built with the help of Blender that we uploaded on Torch, but the application has since then been discontinued. With our extra time, we also created a more simplified AR experience with the help of Facebook's SparkAR by creating a filter that we hoped would connect the Northwestern student body.
